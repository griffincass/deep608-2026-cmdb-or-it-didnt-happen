Account & Access Control / Who Has the Keys?

CIS 5 & 6 · THE INSIDER

The Safeguards

  • Inventory every account (5.1)
  • Disable dormant accounts (5.3)
  • Require MFA on admin accounts (6.5)

How to Measure CAS v8.1

Accurately listed accounts / Total authorized accounts
CIS 5.1 - Is your account inventory accurate?
Admin accounts with MFA / Total admin accounts
CIS 6.5 - How many admin accounts require MFA?
Executive Scenario
Boss: "T-Mobile insider breach. Axios npm package hijacked via social engineering. Are we at risk?"
With CIS 5 & 6: Admin accounts separated, access grants logged, dormant accounts disabled. You can show the last review date. Without it: No way to know who has access to what.
The Insider made practical. CAS metrics verified from cas.docs.cisecurity.org. CIS 5.1 Accuracy: M8/M6 (accurately listed accounts vs total authorized). CIS 6.5: M2/M1 (admin accounts with MFA vs total admin accounts). T-Mobile and Axios npm compromise are both real 2026 events.

Incident Response / Have the Plan Before the Storm

CIS 17 · THE STORM

The Safeguards

  • Designate personnel to manage incidents (17.1)
  • Maintain contact info for reporting (17.2)
  • Document a process for reporting incidents (17.3)

How to Measure CAS v8.1

Components documented / Required components
CIS 17.1 - Is your IR designation complete and current?

CIS 17 safeguards are procedural - they measure whether documentation exists, is complete, and was reviewed within 12 months.

Executive Scenario
Boss: "Medusa ransomware encrypted three orgs in 72 hours. What happens if we get the call at 2am?"
With CIS 17: Named incident commander, current contact list, documented process. The difference between a 72-hour recovery and a 72-day recovery is whether the plan existed before the call.

The storm was predictable. Your readiness is a choice.

CAS metrics verified: 17.1 is M2/3 (components documented / 3 required components). 17.2 and 17.3 are binary existence checks plus component completeness. CIS 17 is procedural — it measures whether docs exist and are current, not operational metrics. Medusa ransomware is real — zero-days, encrypts within days of access.
